Business in the Philippines is diverse, with key sectors including retail, manufacturing, real estate, and services. The country's strategic location and young population make it an attractive investment hub. While challenges like infrastructure and regulations exist, the business climate is strengthened by growing foreign investment, particularly in BPO, technology, and renewable energy. Government efforts to improve business conditions further boost its appeal.





Philippines more Borrowing Slower Growth

The Philippines' debt-to-GDP ratio rose to 62% in Q1 2025, the highest in two years, due to frontloaded government spending. This exceeds the 60% global threshold and the ₱16.68 trillion debt raises concerns about fiscal stability. Finance Secretary Ralph Recto expects the ratio to hit targets by year-end, while economists stress the need for tax and fiscal reforms. Growth may improve with easing inflation, better tax collection, and possible interest rate cuts.


Forex reserves dip to $104.6 billion in April

The Philippines, gross international reserves (GIR) fell to $104.6 billion in April, down from $106.7 billion in March, as the government withdrew from its foreign currency deposits to meet debt obligations and expenses. Despite the drop, reserves were still up 0.8% year-on-year. BSP foreign investments declined 3.2% amid global market volatility linked to U.S. policies. Economist Michael Ricafort noted that inflows from remittances, BPOs, exports, and tourism may help sustain GIR, though risks from the trade deficit and debt payments remain. The reserves still cover 7.2 months of imports and are 3.6 times the short-term external debt well above international adequacy standards.


ACEN, earns P1.95 billion in Q1

ACEN Corp. reported a 28% drop in Q1 net income to ₱1.95 billion, down from ₱2.7 billion last year, due to lower domestic power generation and declining Wholesale Electricity Spot Market (WESM) prices. Revenues fell 21% to ₱7.8 billion. Philippine renewable energy output declined 14% to 489 GWh amid typhoon-related repairs, while WESM rates hit a two-year low. However, ACEN’s total renewables output rose 3% to 1,680 GWh, driven by international operations. The company plans to raise up to ₱30 billion through a stock rights offering to support expansion, aiming for 20 GW in renewables capacity by 2030.











Filipinos spend more time on lending apps BSP urges smarter borrowing

Filipinos spent over 1.54 billion seconds—or an average of 12 minutes per user per month—on digital lending apps in 2024, up 16% from 2023, according to Digido. Personal loan apps made up 76.4% of activity, followed by buy now, pay later (21.4%), and installment loans (2.2%). App downloads rose from 89.7 million to 127.7 million, with unique users jumping to 67.8 million. Digido attributes the growth to demand for easier access to loans, especially from users who may not qualify with stricter traditional lenders. The BSP welcomed the trend as a step toward financial inclusion but emphasized the need for stronger financial literacy and consumer protection.


DA remains optimistic on record palay harvest

Despite flat first-quarter output, the Department of Agriculture (DA) remains optimistic about hitting its record 2024 palay production target of 20.46 million metric tons (MT), surpassing last year’s 20.06 million MT. The Q1 harvest was nearly unchanged at 4.69 million MT, with planted area down 2% but offset by a record-high yield of 4.09 MT per hectare. The DA credits improved farm productivity and state programs, including the double dry cropping season, for boosting yield. Delayed planting in areas like Cagayan Valley is expected to reflect in Q2 output.
