Meet Pope Leo XIV: Mathenmatician, multilingual, with roots in the Americas

Cardinal Robert Francis Prevost, now Pope Leo XIV, made history as the 267th pope and the first American pontiff. Born in Chicago to a French-Italian father and Spanish mother, the 69-year-old is a polyglot fluent in several languages. He holds dual U.S.-Peruvian citizenship after serving over 20 years in Peru, including as Bishop of Chiclayo. A member of the Augustinian order, Leo XIV studied mathematics and canon law and was ordained in 1982. Before his election on May 9, 2025, he served as Prefect of the Dicastery for Bishops and was a familiar figure in Saint Peter’s Square.


ICC presecutor: No proof showing 'perceived bias' of judge

MANILA, Philippines — ICC prosecutor Karim Khan dismissed the defense’s claim of bias by two judges in the case against former president Rodrigo Duterte, saying prior legal rulings don't imply prejudice. The defense requested Judges Alapini-Gansou and Liera to excuse themselves from ruling on jurisdiction, but the Pre-Trial Chamber denied the move, citing improper procedure. Khan emphasized the impartiality of ICC judges and noted that previous decisions were made without defense input. Meanwhile, DOJ Secretary Remulla said he and co-respondents would coordinate their legal defense against a related complaint filed by Sen. Imee Marcos.


LOOK: 75th Europe Day celevration in Manila

MANILA, Philippines — EU Ambassador Massimo Santoro led the Europe Day celebration in Manila on Thursday, commemorating the 75th anniversary of the “Schuman Declaration.” Joined by Acting Foreign Affairs Secretary Theresa Lazaro and Apostolic Nuncio Charles Brown, Santoro emphasized unity in diversity and the EU’s commitment to dialogue, resilience, prosperity, and global values, even amid global challenges.











GUIDON's Foreword 2025 interview with Sonny Matula

MANILA, Philippines — Senatorial candidate Sonny Matula shares his platform in Foreword 2025, a youth-led interview series by Ateneo's The GUIDON that highlights issues based on the UN’s 2030 Sustainable Development Goals. The fifth episode airs May 9 at 6 p.m., co-presented by Rappler and other student media organizations, aiming to amplify youth and marginalized voices ahead of the 2025 midterm elections.


Pope Francis backed him when he took on a president. Now he's voting in the conclave

Cardinal Pablo Virgilio David, recently appointed by Pope Francis, embodies the Church’s shift toward serving the marginalized. Known for opposing Duterte’s drug war and memorializing slain teen Kian Delos Santos, David faced threats and sedition charges but was supported by the Pope. He acknowledges the Church’s waning political influence but believes it should still offer moral guidance. Amid declining attendance and past scandals, David says the Church must rebuild trust through humility and by staying close to the people.
