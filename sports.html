Sports in the Philippines are a key part of the nation's culture, with basketball being the most popular. Boxing, led by icons like Manny Pacquiao, also has a strong following, while volleyball and football are gaining popularity.
The success of Filipino athletes in international competitions, like the Southeast Asian Games, fuels national pride. Leagues like the PBA and events like the UAAP bring communities together, making sports a constant source of entertainment and unity.





Alex Eala to play with Coco Gauff in Italian Open doubles

After an early exit in the singles tournament of the Italian Open, Filipina tennis ace Alex Eala will now shift her focus to the doubles tournament where she will play with 2023 US Open champion Coco Gauff. Gauff, 21 years old, is ranked no. 3 by the Women's Tennis Association. Prior to this, Gauff fell to world no. 1 Aryna Sabalenka in the Madrid Open. Gauff also ruled the Italian Open singles tourney back in 2023. The 19-year old Eala, meanwhile, recently reached a career-high no. 70 in the world rankings. In Madrid, she fell to world no. 2 Iga Swiatek in the Round of 64.


LA28 to use two stadiums for Olympics opening ceremony

The 2028 Summer Olympics will blend the past and present as Los Angeles becomes the first city to feature two major stadiums in the opening ceremony. The Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum, which has hosted three opening ceremonies, and SoFi Stadium will both play key roles in the July 14 event. The Coliseum also hosted the 1932 and 1984 Olympics, while SoFi, home to the NFL's Rams and Chargers, has hosted major events like the Super Bowl. The 2028 Paralympic Games' opening ceremony will be held at SoFi Stadium on August 15.


NBA: Rockets, Fred VanVleet extend deal deadline —report

The Houston Rockets and guard Fred VanVleet are pushing back Friday's deadline for the $44.9 million team option in his contract, ESPN reported Wednesday. The two sides now have until June 29 to decide whether to exercise the 2025-26 option or perhaps negotiate a longer-term deal, per the report. VanVleet, 31, has played the past two seasons in Houston following seven seasons in Toronto, where he captured an NBA title in 2018-19 and made the All-Star team in 2021-22. VanVleet averaged 14.1 points, 5.6 assists, 3.7 rebounds and 1.6 steals in 60 games (all starts) for the Rockets this season. He finished third on the team with 159 3-point buckets as Houston ended a four-year playoff drought. Undrafted in 2016 out of Wichita State, VanVleet has averaged 14.9 points, 5.7 assists, 3.4 rebounds and 1.4 steals in 550 career games (401 starts).











Kieffer Alas sets sights on US after playing for DLSZ

UAAP Season 87 juniors basketball Most Valuable Player and Gilas Pilipinas Youth standout Kieffer Alas is heading to the United States. Alas on Wednesday posted his message to his alma mater De La Salle Santiago Zobel, which he represented in the UAAP. "Words can’t express my gratitude. Thank you for loving me not because of but in spite of everything that has happened. As I enter my senior year in [the United States], I couldn’t be more grateful to Zobel for their guidance and support. Such an honor to represent the green and white. Heck of a ride, always thankful that God led me to this school."


NBA: With Steph Curry out, which Warriors will step up in Game 2 vs. Wolves?

Stephen Curry is out for Game 2 of the Western Conference semifinals against the Minnesota Timberwolves due to a left hamstring injury. The Warriors, leading 1-0 in the series, aim to secure a second road victory without their star player. Curry, who was injured in Game 1 after scoring 13 points in 13 minutes, is expected to miss at least a week. Despite Curry’s absence, coach Steve Kerr remains confident in the team’s ability to perform, although Curry hasn’t missed a playoff game since 2018. The Warriors are 8-3 in playoff games without him, compared to 104-51 with him.
